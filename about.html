--- layout: about title: About Me --- # Hi! I am Eric Lee. Nice to see you here!

- A Small Profile About Me -

Background

I was born in Maple Ridge to my Taiwanese parents that immigrated to Canada. I moved to Seattle when I was just about to start school. Then life made a twist, my parents decided on moving to Hsinchu where I lived for another 10 years. I returned back to Vancouver to attend the University of British Columbia and have happily lived here since then.

Education

I am enrolled as a MSc graduate student in Bioinformatics at the University of British Columbia in Vancouver. My studies are interdisciplinary in medicine, statistics, and computer science.
I am also a BSc alumni in Computer Science, Microbiology and Immunology at UBC, as well as a BEng exchange alumni in Computer Science and Computer Engineering at Waseda University in Tokyo.

Languages

I am a native speaker in English, Mandarin Chinese, Taiwanese. I learned Japanese in high school and have been certified with JLPT N1. I learned Japanese Sign Language (JSL) during my exchange in Japan.

Work Life

I currently work as a graduate student researcher at BC Cancer Research Centre in Vancouver. I focus on applying computer vision, machine learning, and Bayesian modeling to cancer imaging data.
I have hired as a research assistant in the Wasserman Lab at BC Children's Hospital Research Institute, and the Hallam Lab at Life Sciences Institute during my undergraduate years.

Hobbies

I love travelling, taking photos and videos, even just strolling down Main Mall of UBC. I enjoy playing badminton and volleyball, playing the violin, and riding a uni/bi-cycle. Pokemon was one of the first things I loved in my life. I am currently intoxicated with online clips and videos.
